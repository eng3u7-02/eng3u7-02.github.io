The Love Song of J. Alfred Prufrock

Ragged Claws

Silent woods
Lonely trees
No one around—no one next to me
Little boy
Warm summer breeze

When he saw me
he loudly screamed
Discontent
Anger
Deep envy

There and then
My rough hands
Found their place
Around his neck

The earth accepted him
The wind tucked him in
The world had one less boy
To hate a monster like me

I would never be
Any less monstrous than he

Little Frankenstein
Why do you upset me?
Could you not have
Held that last scream?

Innocent angel
Your glorious features
Defy my existence
If you are diamond
Then I am coal

Wherever I go
They make me to be less
Of a human than they

Should I be the only
Deprived of attributes that
Make you beautiful
And lack thereof
Make me beastly?

Afraid

What is it I seek on this path alone,
When I cannot feel this body I call my own?
From a distance, I spot a tall stranger
Suddenly, I feel a sense of danger

Straight up ahead there is such darkness,
As if there was no light to harness.
Yet towards the darkness I am drawn,
And I see I am nothing but a pawn.

The stranger! I remember as I look around,
But he is nowhere to be found.
My eyes wander for anything I can find,
And then I feel a shiver up my spine.

I look ahead, and again there he is,
Staring at me with hollow eyes of his.
Through the darkness, I find him in the centre,
Beckoning me, with his hand, to enter.

Rationale:

The poem “Afraid” represents the fear of death of the speaker from “The Love Song of J. Alfred Prufrock”, who claims to have seen the “eternal Footman”, the Grim Reaper. Aside from the fear of death, this poem also portrays the fear of the unknown and the unwillingness to take risks. The speaker appears lost after death, wandering with no goal in mind, and eventually finding a path to the darkness and the unknown. The tall stranger spotted by the speaker is the representation of the “eternal Footman” who has come to take the unwilling speaker to the afterlife, as daunting as death may be.
